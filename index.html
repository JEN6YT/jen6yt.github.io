Machine Unlearning Techniques @ McAuley Lab, UCSD

with Prof. Julian McAuley

In this project, we explored various machine unlearning techniques to mitigate model bias and unethical knowledge in large models. We implemented and compared different unlearning methods on benchmark datasets, analyzing their effectiveness while maintaining model performance.

Causal Deep Learning for Optimizing Aggregated Functions in User Growth

with Dr. Will Y. Zou and Shuyang Du

This study investigated causal meep learning methods to optimize costly marketing campaigns and maximize user engagement. We implemented novel algorithms including direct ranking model with neural network. We also investigated and compared our treatment effect optimization methodology with existing algorithms such as R-Learner and Causal Tree and they have SOTA performance on cost-efficiency under real-world business constraints.

Hebbian Learning and Local Inhibition @ Social AI Lab, UofT

with Prof. William Cunningham

This project investigated biologically plausible neural network architecture. Inspired by Hebbian rule, Locally Competitive Algorithm, predictive codeing, we developed activation and weight update rule based on minimizing a pre-defined "energy" function (i.e. aggrgated reconstruction error of the network for all layers). We evaluated its performance on standard benchmarks such as MNIST, Cifar10, and ImageNet, and it achieved SOTA performance. In addition, we included visualizaiton techniques to verify model interpretability and biologically plausibility.

Predicting and Analyzing Methane Emission Levels @ Risk Lab, UofT

with Prof. Luis Seco

Methane Emission Drivers and Baseline Calculations

Yuntong Zhang, Behzad Azadie Faraz, Luis Seco
In ADIA Lab.

pdf code

This study presents a comprehensive analysis of feature importance selection methods aimed at identifying the key drivers of methane emissions globally. We identify 8 critical features among 58 contributing most to methane emissions. In addition, we apply four machine learning models, Support Vector Machine, LightGBM, XGBoost, and Random Forest, to estimate methane level across various countries. Moreover, we determine and anlayze the local and global trend for methane emission across 220 countries and identify the top countries that have the steadiest and the most decrease as well the most increase in methane emission in the recent 30 years.

Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) and Second-order Optimization @ Multimedia Lab, UofT

with Prof. Mahdi Hosseini

We explored SSL such as Barlow Twins and DINO with second-order optimization techniques such as KFAC, TEKFAC, and AdaFisher on the task of medical image classification and other downstream tasks. We created a benchmark by comparing those SSL and optimization methods on the downstream tasks.
